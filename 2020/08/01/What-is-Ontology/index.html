What is Ontology?

The word

Explanation from video What is ontology? Introduction to the word and the concept by
Kent Löfgren.

“Onto”: existence, or being real
“Logia”: science, or study

The word is used in two settings:

  • Philosophy context: ontology is the study of what exists, in general.

    • e.g. what are the fundamental parts of the world?
    • Two branches of ontology:
      • Ontological materialism: material things are more real than human minds. Reality exists regardless of human observers
      • Ontological idealism: immaterial phenomenon are more real than material things. Reality is constructed in the mind of the observer.

  • Non-Philosophical context: an ontology is the description of what exist specifically within a determined field.

    • Focus on naming parts and processes, and grouping similar ones into categories.
    • Example:
      • Social ontology, describe society and its different parts and processes, with the purpose of understanding and describing the underlying structures that affect individuals and groups.
